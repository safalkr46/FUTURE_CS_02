Phishing Indicators Assessment

Common Indicators Observed

1. Impersonation of Trusted Services

Attackers attempted to imitate legitimate organizations including Microsoft, PayPal, and internal HR departments.

Example:
support@microsoft-security-alert.com

⸻

2. Fear & Urgency Tactics

Several emails attempted to create panic using statements such as:

* “Your account will be suspended”
* “Immediate verification required”
* “Urgent wire transfer needed”

⸻

3. Suspicious Hyperlinks

Embedded URLs redirected users to fake login or verification pages.

⸻

4. Generic Greetings

Examples included:

* Dear User
* Dear Customer
* Hello Employee

⸻

5. Business Email Compromise Attempt

One sample attempted to pressure the finance department into performing a confidential wire transfer.

⸻

Conclusion

The phishing samples relied heavily on social engineering techniques designed to manipulate users into revealing sensitive information or performing unauthorized actions.
