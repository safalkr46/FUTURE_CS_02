# Phishing Email Detection & Awareness System

---

## Executive Overview

Phishing attacks remain one of the most dangerous cybersecurity threats targeting individuals, businesses, and organizations worldwide. Cybercriminals commonly use fake emails, spoofed domains, malicious links, and deceptive attachments to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, financial details, and confidential organizational data.

This project focused on analyzing phishing email samples, identifying suspicious indicators, evaluating potential risks, and creating awareness documentation to help users recognize and avoid phishing attacks.

**The assessment included:**

- Analysis of phishing and suspicious email samples
- Email header and sender investigation
- URL and domain inspection
- Risk classification
- Awareness and prevention guidance

The main objective of this project was to simulate a real-world phishing analysis process and demonstrate practical cybersecurity investigation and reporting skills.

---

## Project Objectives

The key objectives of this project were:

- Identify phishing indicators within email samples
- Analyze suspicious sender information and email headers
- Detect spoofed domains and malicious URLs
- Classify emails based on security risk
- Create awareness documentation for users
- Recommend preventive cybersecurity measures

## Methodology

The following approach was followed during the assessment:

1. Public phishing email samples were collected for analysis.
2. Common phishing indicators such as spoofed domains, urgent language, suspicious links, and malicious attachments were identified.
3. Email headers were inspected using online analysis tools.
4. Email samples were categorized into **Safe**, **Suspicious**, and **Phishing** classifications.
5. Findings and awareness documentation were prepared along with prevention recommendations.

---

## Major Findings

### Domain Impersonation

Several phishing emails used domains designed to imitate trusted organizations and services.

**Examples:**
```
paypa1-security.com
micr0soft-alerts.com
```

---

### Social Engineering Techniques

Many phishing emails used urgency, fear, and account suspension warnings to pressure recipients into immediate action.

---

### Suspicious URLs

Multiple emails contained links redirecting users to fake login or credential collection pages.

---

### Malicious Attachments

Some suspicious emails included ZIP attachments that could potentially deliver malware or harmful payloads.

---

### Email Header Analysis

Header analysis revealed several suspicious indicators including:

- SPF validation failures
- Missing DKIM signatures
- Suspicious relay servers
- Spoofed sender information

| GitHub Login Notification | **Low** | Legitimate email — no malicious indicators detected |
| Business Email Compromise Sample | **High** | Financial manipulation and urgent payment requests |

---

## Security Recommendations

The following cybersecurity measures are recommended:

- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Carefully verify sender email addresses
- Avoid clicking unknown links or downloading suspicious attachments
- Conduct regular employee cybersecurity awareness training
- Use anti-phishing email filtering solutions
- Report suspicious emails immediately
- Keep browsers and systems updated regularly
